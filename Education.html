Learning is the key to success.

I am a Junior at Brigham Young University in the Finance Program. So far, preparation to be accepted into this program has taught me the strenghts and weaknesses that I have, as well as a littl bit about how the real world works. Let me explain.

I never knew anything about business until I set my eyes on it as a career path. Through the prerequisite classes, I have learned and mastered the basics in many of the most important fields of business: economics, accounting, global supply chain management, information systems, and finance. These classes have opened my eyes into the world of financial statements, supply and demand, web development, product sourcing, and the time-value of money. Throught these introductory experiences, I have chosen to follow the path of finance, because of the emphasis on efficiency and looking towards the future. Previously to Finance, I wanted to be a mechanical engineer - focusing on modeling, equations, and ensuring the best possible outcome with the fewest possible inputs. Finance incorporates the same ideas, but into a business which is really attractive to me.

I hope to graduate BYU in December 2025 and use my knowledge I have gained to be an investment banker.
